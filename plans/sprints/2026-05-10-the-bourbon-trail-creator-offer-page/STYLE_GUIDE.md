# The Bourbon Trail Creator Page Style Guide

Source reference: user-provided Instagram profile screenshot for
`@thebourbontrail`.

## Brand Signals

- Clean, bright Instagram profile presentation, not a dark whiskey-club mood.
- Circular coral, orange, and pink gradient logo with white bottle and glass
  silhouettes.
- Content positioning: Bourbon Whiskey Content.
- Bio cues: Kentucky distilleries, USA whiskey, barrels, pours.
- Highlight labels: On the Trail, Finds, Shoutouts.
- Feed direction: warm distillery and bottle photography with amber barrel
  warehouses, glass highlights, bottle closeups, and bright outdoor contrast.

## Palette

- Page background: warm white `#FFFDF8`.
- Surface: white `#FFFFFF`.
- Text: charcoal `#17171B`.
- Muted text: warm gray `#5F554D`.
- Border: pale peach `#F3D1C0`.
- Coral: `#F24E72`.
- Sunset orange: `#FF7647`.
- Bourbon amber: `#F2A51E`.
- Button fallback accent: burnt orange `#D4522F`.

## Visual System

- Use a light page, warm borders, and subtle brown shadows so it feels closer
  to their Instagram profile than the Black Shirt black/gold page.
- Use the logo as the primary creator identifier with a gradient ring.
- Use restrained gradient accents only where they echo the profile logo:
  top rule, logo ring, offer-card rule, and primary CTAs.
- Keep offer cards simple and readable with two balanced annual plan choices.
- Keep the manual fallback closed by default and visually secondary.

## Layout Notes

- Hero: creator logo plus BarrelBook mark, followed by the co-brand line
  `thebourbontrail x BarrelBook`.
- Content chips should borrow from their profile highlights and bio: On the
  Trail, Finds, Kentucky distilleries.
- Use the existing BarrelBook bottle-scanning video instead of static app
  screenshots, so the creator page feels more active and less like recycled
  product stills.
- Preserve the existing promo mechanics: `/thebourbontrail` redirects to
  `/p/thebourbontrail`, Plus and Pro deep-link to their own campaign slugs, and
  App Store codes stay inside the manual fallback disclosure.
